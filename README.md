# HR Analytics & Employee Attrition Dashboard

## Project Overview

This project analyzes employee attrition trends using the IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition & Performance Dataset. The objective of the project is to identify the key factors influencing employee turnover and support data-driven HR decision-making using Excel, PostgreSQL, and Power BI.

The dashboard provides insights into employee demographics, overtime impact, salary distribution, job satisfaction, department-wise attrition, and workforce retention trends.

---

## Business Problem Statement

Employee attrition is one of the major challenges faced by organizations due to its impact on productivity, recruitment costs, and workforce stability. This project focuses on analyzing employee behavior patterns to identify the major drivers contributing to attrition, including overtime, salary levels, age groups, and job satisfaction.

The goal of the analysis is to help HR teams improve workforce retention strategies and organizational planning through analytical insights and interactive visualization.

## Key Insights

* Overall employee attrition rate was 16.12%
* Research & Development recorded the highest attrition
* Overtime employees showed significantly higher turnover
* Employees aged 26–35 contributed most to attrition
* Lower salary groups experienced higher employee resignations
* Lower job satisfaction levels were associated with increased attrition

---

## Business Recommendations

* Reduce excessive overtime workload
* Improve employee engagement initiatives
* Strengthen retention programs for employees aged 26–35
* Improve compensation strategies for low salary groups
* Conduct regular employee satisfaction assessments
* Focus retention efforts on high attrition departments

## Conclusion

This project successfully identified the major workforce factors contributing to employee attrition using Excel, PostgreSQL, and Power BI. The dashboard provides actionable insights that can support HR teams in improving employee retention, workforce planning, and organizational decision-making.
